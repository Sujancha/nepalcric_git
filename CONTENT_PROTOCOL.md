# NepalCric — Content Build Protocol (v2)
## Refined from Rohit Paudel session. Apply to every player profile. Non-negotiable.

---

## STANDING EXECUTION STANDARDS
*(Applied before a single word is written)*

These are not suggestions. Every violation found in the Rohit Paudel session has been codified here.

### PROSE QUALITY
- **No cheap fragmented prose.** Proper paragraphs throughout — minimum 3–5 sentences each. The prose must breathe and develop, not flicker.
- **Von Restorff isolation: maximum 4 per piece.** Single isolated lines exist only when they are genuinely, undeniably earned — a gut-punch, a statistical revelation, a one-word emotional disclosure. Count them. If you exceed 4, cut the weakest ones back into paragraphs.
- **HYPER-DETAILED NETFLIX DOCUMENTARY DEPTH (CRITICAL OBLIGATION):** Every section must be extensively detailed, immersive, and heavy with sensory information (sweat, sound, tension). The writing must be so detailed that "no one else has ever written anything like it." Generic summaries, rushed paragraphs, and one-liners are strictly forbidden. You must make the reader feel they are inside the exact second the event happened. Expand on the psychology, the environment, and the stakes.
- **Statistics are weapons, not data.** Never write "top run-scorer." Always write "279 runs in 7 matches, team last place, alone." The human cost of the statistic is the story. Exact numbers always. Vague claims never.
- **No translated-English sentence rhythms.** Think in Nepali. The Connective Tissue Rule applies: flowing gravity through verb-nesting, not "र" chains.
- **Never name emotions.** Show through body, environment, and sensation. "उसको हत्केलामा पसिना थियो" not "उ डराएको थियो।"

### DEVANAGARI COMPLIANCE
- **All user-facing prose in pure Devanagari.** No Latin script anywhere a reader will see it.
- **Direct quotes in Devanagari — always.** If a player gave a quote in English, translate it to Devanagari verbatim in meaning. The quote is sacred. Do not paraphrase. Do not clean it. But it must be in Devanagari script.
- **Cricket terms per approved transliteration list:** ओडीआई, टी-ट्वेन्टी, आईसीसी, एनपीएल, सिडब्ल्यूसी — never ODI, T20, ICC in live prose.

### STRUCTURAL REQUIREMENTS
- **Minimum 5 subheadings per player profile.** Each subheading must carry weight — it is a chapter heading, not a label. It must create curiosity about what follows.
- **Bridge transition at the end of every section.** One sentence — in italics, in the voice of a narrator — that pulls the reader forward into the next section. It does not summarise what just happened. It opens what comes next.
- **Breadcrumb planted early, detonated late.** Every Cinematic profile must contain one specific detail — a date, a place, a moment — introduced before the midpoint and paid off in the final third. The reader must feel the click of recognition.

### ACCURACY STANDARDS
- **Verified facts only.** If a fact was not provided by the user or previously verified, it is flagged — not assumed, not inferred.
- **Geography is non-negotiable.** Venue, city, country — verified before writing. (Lesson: Saint Vincent ≠ Dallas. Bayuemas Oval, Kuala Lumpur ≠ generic "Malaysia." Chittagong ≠ anywhere else.)
- **Family status verified before writing.** Father alive or deceased? Mother alive? Any family detail must be confirmed. (Lesson: the father of Dipendra Singh Airee is alive. Writing "बाबुले देख्न नपाएको" without verification was the most serious error of the Rohit Paudel session.)
- **No inferred personal memories or kitchen-table dialogue.** If the player did not say it in a confirmed interview, it does not go in quotes or scene-form. Keep the concrete reality.
- **No vague timeline.** If the year is not confirmed, do not guess. Flag it.

## THE 14-STEP PROTOCOL
*(Sequential. Non-negotiable. Do not skip steps.)*

---

### STEP 1 — IDENTIFY THE TARGET
Ask: *"Which page or player profile are we building or updating today? Is this a new page or an update to an existing one?"*

**If updating an existing profile:** Read the existing file in full before proceeding. Audit what is strong (preserve it), what is factually wrong (kill it immediately), what is missing, and what is structurally weak. Present the analysis to Sujan before moving to Step 2.

Wait for confirmation. Do not assume.

---

### STEP 2 — INFORMATION INTAKE
Ask: *"What new information do you have for this page? Share everything — raw notes, stats, quotes, stories, links, anything. Don't filter it. I'll sort it."*

Collect everything. Do not generate content yet. Do not fill gaps with assumptions.

**Mandatory cross-checks before proceeding:**
- Is the father alive or deceased? Confirmed?
- Is any family anecdote verified or inferred?
- Are all venues/cities named by the user, or are they assumed?

---

### STEP 3 — DEPTH CALIBRATION
Ask: *"How detailed should this content be? Surface / Standard / Cinematic."*

**If Cinematic is chosen:**
- Every section minimum 3–5 full paragraphs
- Netflix documentary depth required
- All 14 steps must be completed in full
- All Standing Execution Standards apply without exception

---

### STEP 4 — FIRST DRAFT

Generate the content. Apply the depth level confirmed in Step 3. If Cinematic, the draft MUST adhere to the "Aarif Sheikh Standard" of heavy drama, profound emotional depth, and visceral storytelling.

**Mandatory before writing:**
1. Identify the In Medias Res hook — maximum tension, name withheld
2. Identify the 5 subheadings and their narrative purpose
3. Identify the Breadcrumb — what detail is planted early and detonated late?
4. Identify the Open Loop — what mystery opens before the midpoint?
5. Identify the Flawed Protagonist moment — one genuine failure, shown as scene
6. Identify the Bridge Transitions — one per section end
7. Count planned Von Restorff isolations — must be 4 or fewer

**Writing rules (The Aarif Sheikh Cinematic Standard):**
- **Netflix Documentary Depth (Extreme Level):** Every emotional beat must breathe. Atmosphere must be thick. Do not rush. Stretch moments of tension. Write long, deeply resonant paragraphs. Never summarize what you can show. Generic one-liners are grounds for failure.
- **Section Length:** Minimum 3 to 5 full, rich paragraphs per section. Do NOT rush the narrative.
- **Sensory Reality:** Exploit smell, sound, weather, and physical weight (e.g., the smell of a border town, the biting cold, the deafening silence of a room).
- Open every section with a moment, never a fact
- Proper paragraphs throughout — no cheap fragmented lines
- Statistics shown as human cost, not as scorecard
- All quotes in Devanagari
- Bridge transition at the end of every section (italicised)
- The "तपाईं" weapon used max 5 times, each targeting a different wound
- The final line contains "तपाईं" and leaves something unresolved

### THE GULSHAN JHA DEEP-DIVE EXPANSION (The Baseline Multi-Act Structure)
*Established during the Gulshan Jha profile rewrite. This is the new minimum standard for comprehensiveness.*

A player's profile cannot just be a summary of their recent stats. It MUST be an "All-in-One" documentary hub capturing everything fans remember. Before finalizing Step 4, force the narrative into a **baseline 6 to 8+ Act Epic Structure**. This is just the starting point for research. Expand as the player's history demands:
1. **The Origins:** The obscure beginnings, the geographical roots, the early unnoticed grind.
2. **The Phenomenon/Arrival:** The sudden breakthrough moment (e.g., Mayor's Cup, U-19 heroics) that shocked the domestic circuit.
3. **The Turning Point:** The specific match or over that proved they belonged at the highest level (e.g., Gulshan's 20 runs off Waseem).
4. **The Hero:** The definitive match-winning performance that achieved a historic milestone for the country (e.g., qualifying for the Asia Cup/World Cup).
5. **The Tragedy/Heartbreak:** The darkest, most painful moment on the world stage (e.g., the 1-run loss in St. Vincent). A 'clutch player' isn't defined by always winning, but by surviving heartbreak.
6. **The Resurrection:** The ultimate evolution. How they bounced back from the tragedy to set new records.

If the draft does not contain these 6 Acts, you have not researched deep enough. Use web search to dig until you find the tragedy and the turning points.

---

### STEP 5 — INTERNAL VALIDATION
Run silently before showing output. Report pass/fail on each:

- [ ] Is every fact sourced from what the user provided — or clearly flagged?
- [ ] Is there any invented quote, paraphrased stat, or assumed detail?
- [ ] Does every section open with a scene — not a summary?
- [ ] Is every direct quote in Devanagari?
- [ ] Are statistics shown as scenes/weapons — not as data lists?
- [ ] Does every section have a bridge transition to the next?
- [ ] Is the family status (father/mother) accurate and verified?
- [ ] Is every venue/city/geography correct?
- [ ] Is the Von Restorff count 4 or fewer?
- [ ] Are there any cheap fragmented lines that should be proper paragraphs?
- [ ] Is the Breadcrumb planted and detonated?
- [ ] Is the Flawed Protagonist shown as a scene — not summarised?
- [ ] Is all prose in Devanagari — no stray Latin script?

---

### STEP 6 — HONEST AUDIT
Present the draft. Then immediately flag:

*"Here is what may need your attention:"*

List anything that is:
- Missing from what the user provided but expected on this page
- Potentially misleading or unverified
- Factually thin or vague
- Geography that has not been independently confirmed
- Family claims that have not been verified
- Statistics presented as claims rather than confirmed numbers
- Emotionally flat — reads like data, not story
- Any English/Latin script in user-facing prose

Be ruthless. Do not protect the draft.

---

### STEP 7 — REWRITE
Incorporate the audit findings. Rewrite the full content — not patches, not edits. A complete rewrite.

**Before finalising:**
- Re-count Von Restorff isolations. Must be 4 or fewer.
- Check every section for bridge transitions.
- Check every statistic — is it a scene or a data point?
- Check every quote — is it in Devanagari?
- Read the last paragraph aloud. Does it make you pause? If no — it is not ready.

---

### STEP 8 — HEADLINE CRAFTING
Generate 5 headline options. Each must:
- Feel like the opening line of a film
- Be written in Devanagari
- Carry the player's identity — not just their role
- Create an itch the reader cannot leave unscratched

Present. Ask Sujan to choose or request variations.

---

### STEP 9 — SUBHEADING AUDIT
Analyse the full content and list every possible subheading that could be extracted — with a one-line description of what each section covers.

---

### STEP 10 — SUBHEADING SELECTION
Ask: *"How many subheadings do you want visible on this page? Which of these serve the story — and which are just filing?"*

**Standard:** Minimum 5. Each must have weight. Each must create curiosity. Finalise based on Sujan's answer. Cut anything that doesn't pull its weight dramatically.

---

### STEP 11 — STRUGGLE TIMELINE CHECK
If player profile: generate the Struggle Timeline.

**Rules:**
- Not career highlights. The hard parts. The years nobody was watching.
- Each entry: dated, scene-first, staccato format
- The darkest entry gets Von Restorff isolation — alone on its own line
- Each entry must feel like a chapter heading in a biography
- Verified facts only — no assumed struggles

---

### STEP 12 — PRAMUKH HATIYAR CHECK
If player profile: generate the Pramukh Hatiyar (Primary Weapon) section.

**Rules:**
- Name the weapon specifically — not "he bats well under pressure"
- Describe its mechanics with technical precision
- Describe its impact with cinematic language
- Back every claim with a specific match moment
- Make it feel dangerous on the page

---

### STEP 13 — CAREER HIGHLIGHTS CHECK
If player profile: generate Career Highlights.

**The test for every highlight:** *"Would a Nepali fan remember exactly where they were when this happened?"*

Each highlight must include:
- The match or moment (with specific venue, opponent, date)
- What was at stake
- Why it matters to the NepalCric story

No highlight makes the list unless it passes the test.

---

### STEP 14 — FINAL PRODUCTION AUDIT

**Four checks. Report pass or fail on each:**

**1. Completeness** — Is this a one-stop profile? Is there anything a fan would want to know that isn't here?

**2. Accuracy** — Is every fact verified or clearly flagged? Zero invented quotes? All venues correct? Family status accurate?

**3. Devanagari Compliance** — All user-facing prose in pure Nepali? All quotes in Devanagari? Zero stray Latin script?

**4. Cinematic Standard** — Read the page as a Nepali cricket fan opening it for the first time with the sound off. Before you read a single word — does the structure, the weight of the sections, the drama of the headings make you feel something?

**The Final Question:**
*"Show this page to a Nepali cricket fan with the sound off. They should feel something before they read anything. If they don't — the atmosphere has failed."*

Has the atmosphere been achieved — yes or no? If no, what specifically needs to change?

**ONLY AFTER ALL 14 STEPS ARE COMPLETE — the page is cleared for production.**
